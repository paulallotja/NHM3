A BIT OF HISTORY

A natural history museum or natural science museum is a scientific institution with natural history collections that serve as references of the natural world, mainly for the scientific community (providing nomenclatural types) but also for the general public as tools for disseminating the natural world. knowledge. The collections of a natural history museum typically include current and historical records of animals, plants, fungi, ecosystems, geology, paleontology, climatology, and much more.


The main function of a natural history museum is to provide the scientific community with specimens and samples, both current and historical, for their research and experiments, with the aim of improving the understanding of the natural world. Some museums have public exhibitions to share the beauty and wonder of the natural world with the general public. These museums are called public museums. Some have non-natural history collections in addition to their main collections, such as collections of objects related to history, art and science.
